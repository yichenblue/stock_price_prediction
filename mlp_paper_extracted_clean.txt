A multi-stage machine learning approach for stock price prediction: Engineered and derivative indices Shaghayegh Abolmakarem a , Farshid Abdi a , Kaveh Khalili-Damghani a , * , Hosein Didehkhani b a Department of Industrial Engineering, South Tehran Branch, Islamic Azad University, Tehran, Iran b Department of Industrial Engineering, Aliabad Katoul Branch, Islamic Azad University, Aliabad, Iran ARTICLE INFO Keywords: Machine learning Arti neural network Stock prediction Price time series Financial forecasting ABSTRACT In this paper, a machine learning approach is proposed to predict the next day ’ s stock prices. The methodology involves comprehensive data collection and feature generation, followed by predictions utilizing Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) networks. We selected 5,283 records of daily historical data, including open prices, close prices, highest prices, lowest prices, and trading volumes from four well-known stocks in the FTSE 100 index. A novel set of engineered and derivative indices is extracted from the original time series to enhance prediction accuracy. Two Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) are proposed to predict the next day ’ s stock prices using the engineered discrete and continuous indices. The case study uses the daily historical time series of stock prices between January 1, 2000, and December 31, 2020. The proposed machine learning approach presents suitable applicability and accuracy, respectively. 1. Introduction Stock price prediction is one of the most challenging problems in the of time series due to its dynamism, nonlinearity, and chaotic nature, in which many parameters often change randomly ( Kumar Chandar, Sumathi & Sivanandam, 2016 ; Ma, Han & Wang, 2021 ). Stock price prediction can be considered as an essential task for investors and institutions, because accurate prediction of the stock price can lead to suitable trade-off among return and risk of in- vestment ( Chen, Zhang, Mehlawat & Jia, 2021 ; Tan, Yan & Zhu, 2019 ). Given the inherent volatility of markets, advanced predic- tive models capable of adapting to ever-changing market conditions are urgently needed. Enhanced prediction accuracy can lead to improved investment decisions, ultimately bene both individual and insti- tutional investors. Portfolio optimization has turned into an important and challenging issue in the of and investment. Portfolio optimization is a good combination of stocks such that the pro of one stock can offset the loss of other stocks ( Mansini, Ogryczak & Speranza, 2015 ). Investors are usually interested in knowing the future status of the stocks they have invested in, and the measures that help them to form the best portfolio ( Wang, Li, Zhang & Liu, 2020 ). Accordingly, stock selection can be considered as a prerequisite and preliminary step for the portfolio optimization. Therefore, combination of portfolio optimization and forecasting theory in the form of using accurate models to predict the stock prices and trends can help the investors to pre-select the proper stocks with potential for greater pro in order to form an optimal portfolio ( Ma et al., 2021 ; Yang, Chen, Li & Tang, 2019 ). Due to the importance of the stock market prediction, many researchers have addressed the issue including stock prices, stock returns, market indices and stock price trend. Furthermore, while numerous studies have explored stock prices, few have focused on the potential of engineered and derivative indices to provide deeper insights into market dynamics and due to our best knowledge there is no certain study in which a complete set of derivative and engineered indices are used to predict the stock price time series rather than direct forecasting of data. The main purpose of this paper is to develop a machine learning approach to predict the next day ’ s stock prices using arti neural networks (ANNs) through a novel and comprehensive set of engineered and derivative indices. A key innovation of this study lies in the extraction of derivative indices from original stock price time series data. By focusing on these engineered indices, we aim to enhance the predictive capabilities of our model. Proposed machine learning approach for stock price prediction The structure of proposed machine learning approach is introduced in this section. 3.1. Proposed machine learning approach structure As mentioned, the main purpose of this paper is to develop a machine learning approach to predict the next day ’ s stock prices using artificial neural networks (ANNs) through a novel and comprehensive set of engineered and derivative indices. The proposed conceptual framework, as shown in Fig. 1 , consists of two main stages. In the first stage, we focus on data collection and the extraction and engineering of indices from the original stock price time series data. This process transforms raw data into meaningful features that enhance predictive accuracy. In the second stage, predictions are generated using Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) networks, leveraging these engineered indices to uncover deeper insights into market dynamics. In the following, the techniques and algorithms used in the proposed multi- stage machine learning approach for stock price prediction are briefly described. 3.2. Conceptual structure of proposed machine learning approach Fig. 1 presents the main stages, i.e., data collection, and feature space generation, and prediction for the machine learning approach for stock price prediction. 3.1.1. First stage: data collection and feature space generation 3.1.1.1. Data collection. Four well-known stocks (including: “ BP p.l.c. ” (BP), “ BHP Group Ltd. ” (BHP), “ GlaxoSmithKline ” (GSK), and “ Voda- fone Group Plc ” (VOD) were selected from FTSE 100. Daily stock price of these stocks for the period from January 1, 2000 until December 31, 2020 were gathered from yahoo finance and considered as datasets of this research. The research period, was chosen due to its historical sig- nificance, encompassing critical market events such as the dot-com bubble and the 2008 financial crisis. The abbreviations of the selected stocks are used for sake of brevity in the following sections. The initial data involves the daily historical series of open prices, close prices, the highest prices, the lowest prices, and the trading volume of assets. This timeframe provides a compre- hensive dataset with 5283 records for each selected stock allowing for a robust analysis of stock price movements across varying economic conditions. After partitioning this dataset into training, validation, and test subsets, we trained our model to learn patterns in the data. The availability of consistent and reliable daily stock price data from repu- table sources further supports the validity of the findings. The FTSE 100 index is a capitalization-weighted index of the 100 most highly capitalized companies traded on the London Stock Ex- change (LSE). By selecting stocks from this index, the research ensures that the chosen companies are among the most significant and most liquid in the UK market, making them representative of the overall market performance. The four chosen stocks, including "BP" represent- ing the oil and gas sector, "BHP" from the mining sector, "GSK" in the pharmaceutical field, and "VOD" from telecommunications, provide a varied representation of the market. This variety enables the research to capture the distinct characteristics and dynamics of different sectors, thereby enhancing the generalizability of the findings across the market while more effectively considering the diverse factors that impact stock prices and market behavior. BP and BHP are highly sensitive to commodity price shifts, leading to greater volatility, while GSK and Vodafone maintain stability due to their strong market positions and consistent demand. The use of logarithmic returns is grounded in theory, providing a normalized measure of price changes over time that stabi- lizes the variance of stock price changes, making the data more suitable for modeling. Logarithmic returns can be easily summed over time, allowing for more straightforward calculations of cumulative returns and facilitating interpretation in terms of percentage changes, which is intuitive for analysis. Technical indicators like RSI, Mo- mentum, TR, ATR, SMA, HMA, ADX, William ’ s %R, and Stochastic Oscillator provide insights into market sentiment, trend strength, vola- tility, and potential reversals, enabling the model to adapt to varying market conditions and identify trends, support/resistance levels, over- bought or oversold conditions. Prediction using multi-layer perceptron. Arti Neural Net- works (ANN) is one of the most used methods in time series forecasting ( Erzurum Cicek & Kamisli Ozturk, 2021 ). Multi-layer Perceptron (MLP) is a kind of arti neural network which is relatively easy to imple- ment. MLP has the ability to learn the complex relationship between the input variables and target due to the presence of activation function in each layer. The Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) was selected as the prediction model for this study based on several critical criteria. First, MLPs are particularly effective at capturing the complex, non-linear relationships inherent in time series data, which is crucial for accurate stock price prediction. Additionally, as highlighted in Ayyıldız (2023) , MLPs have demonstrated strong performance in predicting stock market movements, making them suitable for this research. The flexibility of MLPs to incorporate engineered and derivative indices as input features enhances their applicability, allowing for a more nuanced analysis of market dynamics. Furthermore, the study includes a comparative anal- ysis with other machine learning models, such as logistic regression and support vector machines, to validate the effectiveness of the MLP and ensure a comprehensive evaluation of the predictive capabilities of different approaches. The computational cost associated with Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) architectures is typically lower than that of more complex machine learning models, such as Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) net- works. This reduced computational burden enables faster training times and decreases resource requirements, making MLPs particularly well-suited for real-time applications in trading environments. The following general objectives may be considered in multilayer perceptron training: • Regression . In this case, the network regresses the independent variables, provided by the inputs, onto the dependent variable. The input variables can be categorical or numeric types, however, for regression ANNs, the dependent variable is numerical. The regres- sion method will produce an estimated value of a dependent vari- able, which minimizes the mean-squared error. • Supervised classification . In this case, the output variable is a cat- egorical variable (or binary) and an input vector or pattern is to be trained to select a class from a list of classes. The Taguchi method is implemented in this study to optimize the parameters of the Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) neural network. The key parameters to be optimized are the number of neurons in the hidden layer, learning rate, performance goal, and maximum validation fail- ures. The Taguchi method involves systematically designing an orthogonal array to explore the parameter space. Seven levels of hidden layer sizes (10, 15, 20, 25, 30, 35, and 40) and four levels each for learning rates (0.01, 0.05, 0.1, 0.2), and four levels for performance goals (1e-6, 1e-8, 1e-10, 1e-12), and maximum validation failures (5, 10, 20, 30) was considered. For each experiment, the MLP network is trained with the speci parameter settings, and the performance is evaluated using the Mean Squared Error (MSE) as the performance metric. The signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) is calculated for each experiment to maximize the SNR and achieve optimal performance. The code ana- lyzes the results of the experiments by calculating the mean SNR for each parameter at each level. The combination of optimal levels for all pa- rameters is determined as the overall optimal parameter setting. Conclusion remarks and suggestion for future researches Accurate estimation of future stock prices can be of signi bene to investors in the of stock exchanges. In this paper, ma- chine learning approach designed to predict the next day ’ s stock prices using arti neural networks (ANNs), with a particular focus on engineered and derivative indices. By extracting a novel set of indices from historical stock price data, this research enhances predictive ca- pabilities and provides targeted insights that traditional forecasting methods often overlook. The proposed model not only predicts well- established indicators such as price trends and returns but also in- troduces innovative metrics, including peak and trough predictions and distance measurements between signi price points. The proposed approach had two main stages. In the stage, 5283 records of daily historical series of open prices, close prices, the highest prices, the lowest prices, and the trading volume of assets of four well- known stocks were selected from FTSE 100. Afterward, a novel set of engineered and derivative indices extracted from the original time series for stock price prediction were proposed. In the second stage, two Multi- Layer Perceptron (MLP) were proposed to predict the next day ’ s stock prices using the engineered discrete and continuous indices. The proposed approach in this paper helps the investor in predicting the stock price by presenting a set of new indices extracted from the closing price of stocks. There are several one-day-ahead targets that can be interesting for investors. For example, forecasting the amount of in- crease of the closing price in the next day, the return of the next day, the price trend in the next day. In addition to these well-known indicators, some novel indices such as determining whether the price will be at its peak the next day or not? Predicting whether the price will be in the trough the next day or not? predicting the height of the next peak, predicting the depth of the next trough, predicting the distance from one peak to the next peak, distance from one trough to the next trough, the distance of one peak from the next trough and vice versa, were proposed and developed in this research. These novel indices can be very attrac- tive for the investor. We called such indicators engineered and deriva- tive indices. The aforementioned indices were extracted from daily stock price data to be considered as target variables. In fact, in this research, instead of directly predicting stock prices, engineered indices were used. This study employs engineered and derivative indices because they can enhance predictive capability, provide targeted insights, and improve interpretability while addressing the limitations of traditional fore- casting methods. By leveraging these features, the research aims to contribute to developing more robust and reliable stock price prediction models that can better serve investors in navigating the complexities of markets. Ten different day-ahead indices were de to predict these indices. A set of input variables, including several lagged variables of returns and several technical indicators, were considered. The proposed approach is implemented on four stocks, namely “ BP, ” “ BHP, ” “ GSK, ” and “ VOD, ” that were selected from the daily stock exchange of FTSE 100 between January 1, 2000, and December 31, 2020. The case study involving well-known stocks from the FTSE 100 demonstrates the practical applicability of the proposed model. Investors can leverage these to enhance their trading strategies in actual market con- ditions. Results revealed that by integrating various predictive factors and innovative target variables, investors can gain valuable insights into potential price movements. One of the key innovations of the proposed model is its use of a comprehensive set of engineered and derivative indices extracted from daily stock price time series data. This approach allows for a broader range of input variables, including predictions of trends, peak and trough detection, and distances between signi price points, which have not been previously explored in the literature. By leveraging these indices, the model captures complex and hidden patterns in stock price movements, potentially leading to more accurate predictions than reli- ance on past daily returns. In terms of usability, the proposed approach allows investors to make more informed decisions, ultimately improving their investment strategies. The implications of this research also extend to businesses. Companies can enhance their market strategies and planning by analyzing stock price trends and the factors that drive them. For instance, the insights derived from the proposed model can help organizations determine the optimal timing for stock issuances, effectively manage their capital structures, and re their investor relations strategies. Financial analysts and portfolio managers can leverage the model ’ s predictions to identify undervalued stocks with signi growth potential, enabling them to build portfolios more likely to outperform the market. Additionally, by anticipating fluctuations in stock prices, they can proactively adjust their portfolios to reduce risks and safeguard in- vestors ’ capital. The model ’ s focus on multiple prediction targets pro- vides users with richer insights, enabling better-informed trading strategies. This contrasts with several mentioned in previous research, which primarily focus on predicting next-day returns, potentially limiting its applicability to broader investment strategies. S. This study has several limitations that should be acknowledged. The model primarily relies on engineered and derivative indices derived from historical stock price data, which may restrict its predictive capa- bilities by not incorporating fundamental variables that could enhance accuracy. Secondly, the analysis is based on a limited dataset of four stocks from the FTSE 100, which may affect the generalizability of the to other markets or a broader range of stocks. The suggested approach can be improved in the following aspects in future research. Some fundamental variables can also be considered as the input variables. Also, some base indicators such as gold, oil, gas prices, interest and inflation rates, import and export rates, and GDP may be considered in future research. Extending the prediction window to longer time frames, such as weekly or monthly, could enhance the practical applicability of stock price prediction approaches for investors with different time horizons. Focusing solely on next-day stock prices limits the usefulness of these models to short-term traders. By devel- oping models capable of forecasting stock prices over weekly or monthly intervals, the research would cater to a broader range of investors, including those with medium-term and long-term investment strategies. Expanding the prediction horizon would make the more widely applicable and valuable to the investment community. The proposed approach in this research can be used to preselect stocks to form a portfolio. Therefore, combining this approach with portfolio optimiza- tion problems may be engaging in future research.
